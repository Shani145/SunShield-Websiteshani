Improving agriculture performance through sustainable soil management

Field Trial Overview: Murray State University

Location: Western Kentucky

Principal: Jim Davis, Ph.D.

Crop: BMR Sorghum

The study at Murray State University focused on comparing alternative pasture inoculation methods using Soil Amendment with traditional nitrogen fertilization. The goal was to evaluate grazing performance, Average Daily Gain (ADG), and animal performance on treated pastures.

Materials and Methods

The trial was conducted on a 12-acre plot, divided into four 3-acre test plots, each treated differently:

  • Treatment 1 (N): Nitrogen application (60 units per acre, 3 times during the growing season)
  • Treatment 2 (SG): Soil Amendment at 1 gallon per acre in two split applications
  • Treatment 3 (RM): Raw milk at 2 gallons per acre, applied three times
  • Control (C): No treatment

Results indicated that the Soil Amendment (SG) and raw milk (RM) treatments significantly improved ADG and AUM compared to nitrogen and control, at lower costs per acre.

Additional Studies

Soil Amendment Fall Gain Test

This test examined bull performance on pastures treated with Soil Amendment versus commercial fertilizers. Results showed that Soil Amendment led to increased crude protein and digestibility in forage.

Soil Amendment Rye/Ryegrass/Turnip Trial

In a small-scale trial, heifers grazing on Soil Amendment treated pasture gained 1.88 lbs/day, outperforming the 1.75 lbs/day gain in the control. The treated pasture also showed higher protein, phosphorus, and magnesium content.
